Our Story

In 2002, Dr. Mandla Kawewe witnessed a poisoned river in Luanshya, sparking his journey. In 2006, he founded ZIEJ in Lusaka, starting with a single-room office. From early legal victories in 2009 to launching "Air for All" in 2021, ZIEJ has grown into a continent-wide movement for environmental justice.

Our Profile

Founded in 2006 by Dr. Mandla Kawewe, ZIEJ is a Pan-African NGO headquartered in Lusaka, with offices in Nairobi, Kinshasa, and Pretoria. We work at the intersection of environmental justice, extractive industry governance, and community rights.

Purpose

To advance environmental justice through policy research, litigation, and community mobilization.

Mission

To protect the rights of African communities impacted by environmental degradation.

Vision

An Africa where communities thrive in harmony with the environment.
